## 🔥 Addressing Common Objections

### Objection 1: "Why won't Notion/Confluence just add this?"

**Your Answer:**
"Great question. Three reasons:
1. **Focus:** They're general-purpose knowledge bases. We're laser-focused on decision memory. Our UX is optimized for capture speed, not document creation.
2. **AI-first:** We built AI search from day one. Their search is still mostly keyword-based. Retrofitting AI is hard.
3. **Distribution:** Our browser extension and Slack bot meet teams where they work. They require people to 'go to the tool.'

Plus, we're not competing for document storage. We integrate with Notion - Corteza is the memory layer on top."

### Objection 2: "The market is crowded with knowledge management tools."

**Your Answer:**
"True, but we're in a new category: team decision memory. Knowledge management is broad (wikis, docs, SOPs). We're specific: capturing the 'why' behind decisions.

Think of it this way:
- Notion = where you store documents
- Slack = where you communicate
- Corteza = where you remember why you decided

We're complementary, not competitive. In fact, many customers use us alongside Notion."

### Objection 3: "Isn't this just a note-taking app with AI?"

**Your Answer:**
"No - three key differences:
1. **Structured capture:** We ask 'decision, context, alternatives' not just 'write a note.' This structure makes search more accurate.
2. **Team-wide:** This isn't personal notes. Everyone sees the memory, creating institutional knowledge.
3. **Conversational search:** You don't search for documents, you ask questions and get answers with sources.

It's closer to 'team brain' than 'note-taking app.'"

### Objection 4: "How do you prevent this from becoming another tool people don't use?"

**Your Answer:**
"Great question. Three strategies:
1. **Low friction:** Slack bot and extension mean you log in 10 seconds, no context switching.
2. **Immediate value:** The first time someone asks 'why did we choose X?' and gets an instant answer, they're hooked.
3. **Free AI searches:** 30 free AI searches per month on free tier means they experience the magic before paying.

Our engagement data shows people who do 3+ AI searches become daily active users."

### Objection 5: "You're pre-revenue. What's the path to monetization?"

**Your Answer:**
"We're launching pricing next quarter (already planned). Three tiers:
- Free: 30 AI searches/month (hook)
- Team: $29/month unlimited (core monetization)
- Business: $99/month with enterprise features

We're taking the Slack playbook: land with free, convert with usage-based limit (AI searches), expand with seat-based pricing ($9/additional user).

Based on our beta engagement, we project 8-12% free-to-paid conversion, which would put us at $50K MRR in 12 months with our current growth rate."

### Objection 6: "What if people just use ChatGPT to search their docs?"

**Your Answer:**
"ChatGPT is generic. We're specific. Three advantages:
1. **Context:** We know it's a team decision with alternatives, timeline, outcome. ChatGPT just sees text.
2. **Integration:** We pull from Slack, extension, dashboard automatically. ChatGPT requires manual copy-paste.
3. **Team-wide:** Everyone gets the same answer. ChatGPT is siloed per user.

Plus, we use GPT under the hood for embeddings. We're building the product experience on top of the AI, not competing with the AI."

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Show, don't tell:** Offer to do a live product demo. VCs love seeing the product in action.

2. **Have customer references ready:** "Want to talk to our users? I can intro you to 3 beta customers who love the product."

3. **Be honest about gaps:** "We don't have a technical co-founder yet, but I'm actively looking. Here's my ideal profile..." (shows self-awareness)

4. **Know your numbers:** If you say "50 workspaces," be ready for "What's retention? What's engagement? How many are active daily?"

5. **Have a data room ready:**
   - Financial model (3-year projections)
   - Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period)
   - Cap table (if you've raised before)
   - Any customer contracts or LOIs
   - Product roadmap
   - Competitive analysis

6. **Follow up within 24 hours:** VCs talk to hundreds of founders. Stay top of mind.

## ⚠️ Red Flags to Avoid

**Don't say:**
- "We have no competition" (shows lack of market research)
- "Our tech is proprietary/secret" (most VCs know tech is commodity)
- "We'll sell to [BigCo] in 3 years" (acquisition is not a strategy)
- "All we need is marketing" (implies product isn't working)
- "Everyone is a customer" (shows no focus)

**Do say:**
- "Here are our top 3 competitors and how we differentiate"
- "We use Claude and OpenAI APIs, our moat is product + distribution"
- "We're building a sustainable business, acquisition would be bonus"
- "We need funding to scale what's already working"
- "We're focused on product teams at tech companies, 50-500 employees"
